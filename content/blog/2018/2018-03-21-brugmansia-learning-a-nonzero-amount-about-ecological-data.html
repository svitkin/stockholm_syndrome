Introduction

This post is inspired by the imbalance of my interest in the field of ecology and my understanding of it. Because I have some knowledge of statistics and programming (the work I do is occasionally referred to as “data science” by people less resentful than me), I thought I would do some simplistic data stuff! I’m going to assume anyone reading this has as much knowledge of ecology as I do (i.e. they read a book about it years ago that they liked very much).

Brugmansia

I chose to focus on the plant genus (the step above species in taxonomic rank) Brugmansia for my exploration. It has a really interesting and complicated history worth checking out!

Obtaining Data

Insert information about BIEN

brugmansia_data <- 
  BIEN_occurrence_genus(genus = "Brugmansia",
                        cultivated = TRUE,
                        only.new.world = FALSE,
                        all.taxonomy = TRUE,
                        native.status = TRUE,
                        observation.type = TRUE,
                        political.boundaries = TRUE) %>% 
  filter(!is.na(latitude),
         !is.na(longitude),
         !is.na(date_collected))

brugmansia_data_col <-
  brugmansia_data %>% 
  filter(country == "Colombia")

map("world", fill = TRUE, col = "grey", bg = "light blue")
points(cbind(brugmansia_data$longitude,
             brugmansia_data$latitude),
       col = "red", pch = 20, cex = 1)
